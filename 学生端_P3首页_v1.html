📌 关键词格式说明
→逻辑方向：前因后果、递进、由此得出
/并列：同等重要的两个概念
(vs)对比对象：括号内是被对比的事物
换行新的论点层：每换一行是一个新的角度
future:展望未来：结尾一句预测或趋势
🎯
① 举例法（Example）
用一个具体故事支撑你的观点。不要说"有些人认为…"，说"我有个朋友/我小时候/我见过…"
kid works hard → maths competition
→ first place → parents tell everyone
🔄
② 对比法（Contrast）
用 "(vs ...)" 格式引入对比对象。对比越具体，答案越有说服力。不说"有好有坏"，说"比起……"
(vs toy car) → forget after a week
(vs spending time) → remember for years
👥
③ 主客体（Subject/Object）
明确"谁做了什么"。年龄段分层题尤其有用：young people do X vs old people do Y
young: everything → phone = whole life
old: calling, messaging → basic use
🚀
④ 展望未来（Future）
用 "future: ..." 结尾。一句话预测趋势，让答案有深度。不需要复杂，说一个方向就够了。
future: YouTubers, gamers → internet
future: keep habits as adults
🔍 实战示范 — 一道完整的 Part 3 回答
Q: Should parents buy more toys or spend more time with kids?
关键词：spending time > toys / toys: play a few days → get bored / time: park on Saturday → fly kite, walk around → remember for years / (vs toy car) → forget after a week

展开说："I think spending time matters more than buying toys. Toys are nice for a few days, but kids get bored quickly. I remember my mum taking me to the park every Saturday — we'd fly kites or just walk around. I still remember those days clearly, but I can't even remember most of the toys I had. A toy car? Gone from my memory in a week. But those Saturday afternoons? Still with me."

What are the advantages and disadvantages of being a famous child?
advantage: make money → child movie actor → earn a lot
disadvantage: too young → pressure, attention → no free time → (vs normal kids) hang out, relax
Which outdoor sports are popular with children?
basketball → energy, run around → team sport, play with friends
(vs jogging) → boring alone
near schools → courts → every afternoon, kids playing
What sports can children do on rainy days?
swimming, table tennis → indoors, no weather problem
indoor pool → rainy weekends → full of kids, fun
(vs sunny days) can't play football outside → switch to indoor
What jobs do children want to do when they grow up?
astronaut, doctor, football player → cool, exciting
boys → Messi on TV → 'want to be like that'
(younger kids) superheroes, not real → (older kids) real jobs
future: YouTubers, gamers → internet
Do you know what kind of music children like today?
pop music → Douyin → catchy, easy to sing
one song about animals → every kid singing, two weeks, even teachers
(vs parents) slower, traditional → now: fast, fun pop
What can children learn from stories?
be kind, right vs wrong → still learning about world
story: boy shares food with hungry animal → animal helps later → being nice = good things
(vs just telling 'be nice') → kid won't listen → story makes them feel it
When would parents feel proud of their children?
do something good → high score, win competition
kid works hard → maths competition → first place → parents tell everyone
(hard work vs natural talent) → effort matters more
Should parents buy more toys or spend more time with kids?
spending time > toys
toys: play a few days → get bored
time: park on Saturday → fly kite, walk around → remember for years
(vs toy car) → forget after a week
What is the right age for a child to learn drawing?
three or four → already like holding pen → draw random things
drawing: easy, just paper + crayons
three-year-olds: big heads, tiny bodies → don't care, love it
(vs start at ten) → worry too much about looking good
When should parents encourage children?
trying something new/difficult → not confident, scared
learning to ride bike → keep falling → 'keep trying, you're getting better'
future: grow up more confident → not afraid of new things
What kind of encouragement should parents give?
specific, not just 'good job' → tell exactly what they did well
child builds blocks all afternoon → 'didn't give up, that's great'
(vs 'good boy') → doesn't learn what they did right
How do children become smart at school?
pay attention, do homework → brains learn fast
front row students → ask questions → best grades at end
(active vs passive) → not naturally smarter, just try harder
What habits should children have?
sleep early, eat healthy, read books → growing up, need habits
reading before bed → 10-15 mins every night → learn many words
future: keep habits as adults
What are the benefits of bedtime stories?
relax, fall asleep → active all day, need to calm down
parent reads → little bear story → 10-15 mins → child asleep
(vs watching videos) screen too exciting → story more peaceful
What kind of stories do children like?
magic, adventure → big imagination, exciting things
Monkey King → fly, fight bad guys, funny → kids love him
(younger) simple + pictures → (older) longer, more things happening
At what age should children start learning a foreign language?
five or six → good at copying sounds, learn fast
start at five → simple conversations by eight or nine
(vs start at twelve) → younger kids not shy about mistakes
How do children make friends at school?
playing together → naturally social
lunch / after class → play tag, share snacks → slowly become friends
'do you want to play with us' → that simple
How do children make friends when they are not at school?
activities outside school → curious, open to new people
weekend basketball/dance class → same kids every week → start talking → friends
future: online games → more common
Do you think there are too many subjects for students to learn?
yes → maths, Chinese, English, science, PE, music, art... 8-9 subjects
homework every subject → stay up until eleven → no time to rest
(vs other countries) → choose fewer subjects → less pressure
Should students have physical education and do sports at school?
yes → sit all day, need movement
after PE → sweating but smiling → next class focus better → brain reset
(with PE vs without) → healthier, happier
Should teachers make learning in their classes fun?
yes → young students lose interest easily → boring = stop listening
vocabulary quiz → turn into game, two teams compete → excited, learn more
(vs reading textbook 40 mins) → way better
Why do most children like listening to stories before bedtime?
helps relax → tired but mind still running → soft voice → calm, peaceful world
favourite story about bear/bunny → hear it every night → already know what happens → still feel safe
Why do most children draw more often than adults do?
children: creative, want to express → can't write well → drawing = easiest way
adults: too busy, too tired → work all day → no time for drawing
future: sad → maybe adults pick it up again to relax
What are the benefits of children learning a musical instrument?
brain developing → memory, focus, hand movement all at once
piano: read notes + move both hands + listen → a lot of brain training
even after 1-2 years → more focused in school
future: patience, focus → help no matter what job
Is making study plans popular among young people?
some do, but many don't follow through → lack discipline
before exam → 'study 2 hours every day' → 2-3 days later → phone, social media → plan falls apart
(vs older adults) more self-control → young people: too many distractions
What do young people spend most of their money on?
food, clothes, entertainment → look good, enjoy life
bubble tea every day → concert tickets, games, new clothes → expensive sneakers
(vs older people) rent, groceries, children's education → young: happy right now
What kind of music do young people like?
pop, hip-hop → fast, exciting, easy to find online
music apps → top songs always pop/rap → listen everywhere: bus, gym, studying
some songs → everyone knows lyrics in a few days
(vs older people) slower, traditional music
What books do young people like to read?
novels → fantasy, romance, adventure → exciting, emotional
online novels → millions of stories → time travel, love, fighting monsters
read on phone → lunch breaks, before bed → some stay up late, can't stop
When do old people ask young people for advice?
technology → smartphones, apps, internet → hard for them
WeChat: send photos, video calls, pay with phone → ask younger family
download app, connect wifi → step by step → sometimes forget, ask again
Do you think only old people have time for leisure?
no → everyone needs leisure
young: movies, sports, friends on weekends / children: games, cartoons after school
but old people → most free time → retired
working adults → busiest → work + family → least leisure
Which sports are popular among elderly people?
walking, tai chi, square dancing → gentle, low intensity, safe
morning → parks → tai chi, slowly, peacefully
evening → public squares → square dancing + loud music → social activity, meet friends
(vs young people) intense sports like basketball → old people need slower, safer
Why do old people prefer to live in quiet places?
need rest, peace → body not as strong → loud noise = tiring
after retire → move to small town/countryside → fresh air, less traffic, slower life
wake up naturally, morning walks, no car horns
(vs young people) don't mind noise → enjoy city excitement
What are the differences between old and young people when using apps?
young: new app → figure out in 5 mins → natural
old: find right button → confusing → accidentally close app
also: old → only 1-2 apps (WeChat) / young → dozens of apps every day
(speed + quantity = biggest differences)
How do young and old people use phones differently?
young: everything → social media, videos, shopping, games → phone = whole life
old: calling, messaging, maybe news → basic use
elderly → WeChat → voice message (typing too slow)
young → type fast, stickers, group chats, video calls → completely different
Do old people read more than young people?
depends on what kind → old: newspapers, paper books / young: phone, apps, online
elderly → buy newspaper every morning → read with tea → one hour
young → news on app, social media, online novels → shorter pieces, much faster
both read → style and format completely different
What differences are there between young and old people's music tastes?
young: modern pop, hip-hop → fast, strong beat
old: slower, traditional → old love songs, 80s/90s
family gathering → young plays hip-hop → grandparents think it's noise
grandparents play old songs → young find boring → shaped by generation
Which stage of life is best for learning a language?
childhood → 4-7 years old → brain developing → pick up sounds naturally
kids hear two languages at home → speak both by school age → no studying grammar
(vs adults) → study rules, memorize vocabulary → much more effort
